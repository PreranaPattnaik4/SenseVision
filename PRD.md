# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

# SenseVision

**AI Companion for the Visually Impaired**

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Product Concept  

---

## 1. Product Vision

SenseVision is an AI-powered accessibility companion that helps blind and visually impaired individuals understand, navigate, and interact with both the physical and digital world through natural voice conversations.

Rather than simply identifying objects, SenseVision provides real-time situational awareness—helping users understand their surroundings the way a sighted person naturally would.

Our long-term vision is to evolve SenseVision from a smartphone application into intelligent AI-powered smart glasses that provide hands-free assistance throughout the day.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Blind individuals often rely on family members, caregivers, hotel staff, colleagues, or strangers to describe their surroundings.

Examples include:
* Entering a hotel room
* Choosing food from a buffet
* Shopping independently
* Finding personal belongings
* Navigating unfamiliar places
* Understanding who is nearby
* Accessing websites and online services

Although assistive technologies exist, many only answer simple questions such as:
* "What object is this?"
* "Read this text."

They rarely provide complete environmental understanding or proactive guidance.

---

## 3. Goal

Enable blind individuals to become more independent by providing an intelligent AI companion that understands their surroundings and communicates naturally through voice.

## 5. Product Principles

SenseVision should be:
* Voice-first
* Hands-free whenever possible
* Simple to use
* Context-aware
* Privacy-focused
* Reliable
* Accessible

The user should never be required to navigate complex visual interfaces.

---

## 6. User Interaction

The primary interface is voice.

Example:

**User:**
> "Hey SenseVision."

**AI:**
> "How can I help you?"

**User:**
> "Describe this room."

**AI:**
> "You are in a hotel room. The bed is approximately six feet ahead. The bathroom is to your left. A bedside table with a telephone and water bottle is beside the bed."

---

## 7. User Modes

### Ask Mode (Default)
SenseVision responds only when the user asks.
* Describe my surroundings.
* What is in front of me?
* Where is the bathroom?
* Read this menu.
* Where did I keep my phone?

---

### Smart Auto Mode
SenseVision automatically provides useful information in important situations.
* User enters a room.
* User approaches a staircase.
* Vehicle detected nearby.
* Unexpected obstacle detected.

---

### Hybrid Mode (Recommended)
Automatic safety alerts combined with on-demand assistance.
This reduces unnecessary interruptions while keeping the user informed about important events.

## 8. Core Features (MVP)

### 8.1 Environment Understanding
Describe:
* Rooms
* Buildings
* Streets
* Restaurants
* Offices
* Homes

*Example:* "You are in a conference room. There are five chairs around a table. The exit is behind you."

### 8.2 Object Recognition
Identify:
* Furniture
* Household items
* Electronics
* Bags
* Medicines
* Food items

*Example:* "The object in front of you is a backpack."

### 8.3 Scene Description
Explain what is happening.

*Example:*
* "Three people are waiting in line."
* "There is an empty chair beside you."

### 8.4 Text Reading
Read:
* Menus
* Signboards
* Books
* Documents
* Medicine labels
* Product packaging

### 8.5 Buffet & Restaurant Assistant
Describe:
* Available dishes
* Food categories
* Dessert section
* Beverage section

*Example:* "There are twelve dishes available. Vegetarian dishes are on the left. Desserts are on the right."

### 8.6 Indoor Navigation
Guide users to:
* Bathroom
* Reception
* Exit
* Elevator
* Hotel room

Provide obstacle-aware directions.

### 8.7 Personal Item Finder
Help locate:
* Phone
* Wallet
* Keys
* Backpack
* Water bottle

### 8.8 Safety Alerts
Notify users about:
* Stairs
* Obstacles
* Moving vehicles
* Wet floors
* Open doors
* Low-hanging objects

## 9. Digital Accessibility

### Desktop Assistant (Future)
Understand desktop applications.
* Email
* Documents
* Spreadsheets
* Video conferencing
* Browsers

## 10. AI Memory

SenseVision remembers user preferences.

*Examples:*
* "Remember that my passport is in the top drawer."
* Later: "Your passport is stored in the top drawer."

The long-term goal is to deliver this experience through lightweight AI-powered smart glasses that seamlessly integrate into everyday life, making visual information accessible through natural conversation.
